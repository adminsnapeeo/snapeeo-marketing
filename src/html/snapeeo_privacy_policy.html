Privacy Policy for Snapeeo

Last Updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

Snapeeo ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") operates the Snapeeo photography marketplace platform (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Service.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Service. By accessing and using Snapeeo, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by all the provisions of this Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Registration: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, phone number, profile information, and password.
  • Profile Information: We collect biographical information, professional qualifications (for photographers), user preferences, and account settings.
  • Location Data: We collect location information including city, neighborhood, and GPS coordinates to help match customers with local photographers.
  • Photo Uploads: Both customers and photographers may upload photographs, which may contain metadata, location information, and other information embedded in the images.
  • Payment Information: When you make payments through our Service, we collect payment details which are processed through secure third-party payment gateways. We do not directly store credit card or banking information.
  • Communication Data: Messages, reviews, ratings, and other communications between customers and photographers.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, and device settings.
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, features accessed, time spent, clicks, and browsing patterns.
  • Location Information: We may collect precise GPS location data with your permission to enable location-based services.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We use cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to enhance user experience and analytics.
  • IP Address: Your internet protocol address and related network information.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Information from social media platforms if you connect your account
  • Information from payment processors and verification services
  • Information from third-party analytics providers

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To create and maintain your account, process bookings, and provide photography marketplace services.
  • Communication: To send service updates, booking confirmations, customer support responses, and transactional information.
  • Matching & Recommendations: To match customers with photographers based on location, preferences, and ratings.
  • Payment Processing: To process payments and manage transactions securely.
  • Verification: To verify user identity and prevent fraud and abuse.
  • Marketing: To send promotional materials and marketing communications (with your consent).
  • Analytics: To understand user behavior, improve our Service, and conduct market research.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with legal obligations, law enforcement requests, and contractual requirements.
  • Safety & Security: To detect, prevent, and address fraud, security, and technical issues.

4. Sharing of Information

4.1 Information Shared with Other Users

When using our Service, certain information is shared with other users:

  • Photographers' Public Profiles: Name, profile photo, location, reviews, ratings, and portfolio samples are visible to potential customers.
  • Customers' Booking Information: Photographers see customer location, booking preferences, and basic contact information.
  • Reviews and Ratings: User reviews and ratings are publicly displayed and attributed to users.

4.2 Third-Party Service Providers

We share information with carefully selected third-party service providers who assist in operating our Service:

  • Payment Processors: To process payments securely
  • Cloud Storage Providers: To store photos and user data
  • Analytics Services: To understand usage patterns and improve our Service
  • Email Service Providers: To send transactional and marketing emails
  • Mapping and Location Services: To provide location-based features

4.3 Legal Disclosure

We may disclose your information when required by law or when we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to:

  • Comply with legal obligations, court orders, or government requests
  • Protect our rights, privacy, safety, or property

In the event of merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and any choices you may have regarding your information.

5. Data Security

We implement comprehensive technical, administrative, and physical safeguards to protect your information:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for data in transit
  • Password encryption and secure authentication
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Restricted access to personal information on a need-to-know basis
  • Secure data storage with encryption at rest
  • Employee confidentiality agreements and privacy training
Note: While we implement strong security measures, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide our Service and fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy:

  • Account Information: Retained while your account is active and for a reasonable period thereafter (typically 2 years) for legal and business purposes.
  • Transaction Records: Retained for a minimum of 7 years to comply with financial and tax regulations.
  • Photos & Communications: Retained as long as needed for the Service; deleted upon account termination unless required for legal purposes.
  • Analytics Data: Generally retained for 12-24 months in identifiable form.

You may request deletion of your account and associated data, subject to legal and contractual obligations.

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for recognizing these signals, and our Service does not respond to Do Not Track browser signals.
